The Federal Government and Protestors

Image of Protestors blocking road with banner protesting the Lithum mine Construction, information from "Olalde, M. (2025, July 25). Law enforcement surveilled Nevada lithium mine protesters, according to records. High Country News. https://www.hcn.org/articles/law-enforcement-surveilled-nevada-lithium-mine-protesters-according-to-records/"

Based on over 2,000 pages of law enforcement communication records, obtained by record request forms, High Country news released this article, talking about how the Federal Government, stalked and harassed protesters, simply for living near / being in an indigenous community that opposed the mines creation. Ka'ila Farrell-Smith, a member from the Klamath Tribe in Oregon, grew up deeply involved in the community fight for Indigenous rights. She stated specifically, that members since the 1970's have been repeatedly stalked and harassed by multiple different federal agencies. All later confirmed by declassified government records.

Law enforcement personnel, specifically FBI, Homeland Security, and multiple others privately worked alongside mine security to repeatedly stalk protesters. Tracking everything, from their social media to the video surveillance cameras on their campsite. All said, 10 agencies were monitoring the protesters, that being the FBI, The Bureau of Land Management, Humboldt County Sheriff's Department (in Nevada, not California), Bureau of Indian Affairs, Nevada State Police Highway Patrol, Winnemucca Police Department, and the Nevada Threat Analysis Center. The reason that was given in order to justify this repeated stalking and harassment, was due to the protesters being put under the, "potential terrorists" tag. That giving the federal government full unrestricted access to all information that pertains to you. As a result of the constant stalking, only one protester was arrested. Having a nonexistent threat essentially. In specific, the FBI was tracking Gary McKinney. A member of the Duck Valley Shoshone-Paiute Tribe. He leads annual prayer rides, a practice where Indigenous Community members travel on horseback for hundreds of miles. Which is also protected under the Religious Freedom Act. The FBI tracked his activity, informing other agencies as well of this. The former FBI contractor was not yet licensed to even be doing private investigations under Nevada law, yet still was allowed to continuously harass patrons. Pulling back, we enter the global scale, and that is telling. It shows that 85% of Lithium deposits are on, or near Indigenous Land, meaning that this level of exploitation is not going to end, but is just starting to pick up again. With a projected demand of over 350% by 2040, with the increasing needs by electric vehicles, as well as technology that relys more on this Precocious metal. This peaceful protest was labeled a possible terrorist convergence. A label used to then justify repeated harassment, in a targeted campaign by multiple federal agencies. This shows how governments use scare tactics in order to silence and suppress those that they try to trample over.

A win for the People

Image of The Great Seminole Nation of Oklahoma logo, Information From "Wilkins, J. Futurism. (2025). Tech companies are using insidious tactics to build data centers on Indigenous lands. Futurism. https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/data-centers-tribal-communities"

A historic moment in this nations history, a first of it's kind ever to be done. The Seminole Nation of Oklahoma became the first in the country to ban any data center construction on it's land. This being a unanimous 24 - 0 vote. NDA's or Non Disclose Agreements are a common thing that Technology Companies use. They are used in a manner that typically circumvents any public discussion or recourse, and have been used in ever more increasingly alarming rate to force open data center construction on smaller communities. It is going to be discussed heavily in these topics. In specific to The Great Seminole Nation of Oklahoma, they were approached by a tech company with NDA's with the intent on constructing a Data Center on their land. After which they voted to never allow any data centers on their land in the coming future.

Nation wide, this is becoming increasingly more common. This article in specific references a Two Bulls' Democracy Now! interview, in where she mentions that there are around 103 to 160 proposed hyper scale data centers, that being even larger then regular data centers. A tactic that she names is called the bait and switch. It terms the actions of tech companies getting smaller communities to sign NDA's, with the promises of investments on renewable energy and infrastructure. Then when signed, they are forced to go along with a hyper scale data center. Getting essentially blackmailed on the terms of breaking the NDA and getting sued. It is a malicious tactic that is just incomprehensibly evil. They target these smaller Indigenous Communities because of their access to resources. The same resources they already exploit, they are simply looking for a cheaper alternative to paying what they rightfully owe. Due to the poverty most of these communities face, they are chosen to exploit for cheaper labor, cheaper workers, and when it is all built, they are then used for cheap waste disposal as well. Simply being forced into a colonial cycle that has been ever persuasive for centuries now. A quote that struck me very hard was said by Ash Leitka, in which she states "Communities want clean water, privacy and security, jobs and economic stability .... and hyper scale date centers threaten every negatively impact every single one of those points.", She also calls the AI infrastructure boom a "A falsely generated demand", which is going to be in a "death cycle". All quotes that shows a deeper understanding of this colonial cycle that continues on, just in different forms. The death cycle being literal in the sense of where, a data center pops up, essentially draining and killing the region, leaving it a waste.

The Environmental Footprint of Data Centers in the United States

Image and information obtained from "Siddik, M. A. B., Shehabi, A., & Marston, L. (2021). The environmental footprint of data centers in the United States. Environmental Research Letters, 16(6), Article 064017. https://doi.org/10.1088/17489326/abfba1"

The scale of what is being discussed, is what caught my attention to this paper. After hearing the facts on how much electricity data centers use, as well as the water it uses. I was wondering exactly how much power these data centers used. This paper was exactly what I wanted to find, what I found out is that data centers, in 2018, took exactly 76 terawatt hours of energy per year, to give you some scale, a single terawatt is enough electricity to power a single home, for a 100 million hours. Or the entire state of California for a week and a half. That is being drawn constantly from the US power grid. This paper talks about as well the water it takes, It not only takes most of the subbasins of water in the United States, but All of them, or 2,110 subbasins total. The water consumption was split in two categories, direct and indirect.

Direct water consumption, or the water that is used on location to cool a data center, in reality only takes up one fourth of the total percentages of water usage from these data centers. The other three fourths are categorized as indirect, or water consumed by the power plants generating the electricity that runs the data center. As well as the water used by the water treatment plants, showing that it not only is the data center that is negative. But the entire supply chain as argued by the other topics. Most of the water usage not even being taken into account, as the only water that is tracked in a measurable quantity is the direct water usage that these tech companies abuse. In 2018, the estimated annual water footprint for these date centers reached 513 million cubic meters. Consuming more then the national total of water consumption by citizen, which was 444.3 million cubic meters of water. Almost a 70 million cubic meter water difference. With 90% of the water used by these tech companies coming from US watersheds. Marston, a researcher even acknowledges this by saying that these data centers are in the top 10 water consumers in the nation. With co-author and researcher Shehabi stating that, "this is pretty unusual for an industry". That was 8 years ago now, before the exponential growth and investments made towards data centers. What directly affects Indigenous communities more, that was supported by this paper, is the raw water volume alone, as well as the water scarcity footprint concept. Essentially it measures how scarce water is already in that area. With the higher the number meaning that water, from that specific basin, is harder to extract and harder to come by. The tests that the researchers conducted, as well as averaging the nations totals out. Came to be Indigenous Communities face more then 17.9 cubic meters of water scarcity equivalent compared to each megawatt hour of electricity used. That doesn't sound too bad, however compared to the nation average of 7.1 cubic meters per megawatt hour per community. Flat out proving, that tech companies are going out of there way to already find stressed sub basins, and then attempting to extract as much profit as possible. The paper describes areas that are most in danger, being those Community's that are in the Western and Southwestern United States. As these sub basins are especially vulnerable due to already made colonial laws that dismantled protections for these Indigenous lands where the sub basins sit. So on the local scale, it shows that these data centers require more then 15% - 20% of the available water already in the sub basins. Causing residents of the community to face unreliable water usage. With the companies, not paying any tax on said water. As of 2023, the latest update we have on the total used electricity that these data centers consumed raised to over 176 terawatt hours. More then doubling the expected growth. With this continuous exponential growth, it is estimated we will be at 325 - 580 terawatt hours. A incomprehensible amount of energy.
